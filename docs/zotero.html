Annotated Resources — Five key reads

AI Misinformation and the Value of Trusted News — CEPR / VoxEU

Read the column

Summary: Field-experiment evidence showing how AI-generated misinformation affects readers’ trust and their relative demand for trustworthy news outlets.

Why it matters: Offers empirical support that misinformation changes market behavior for news—helpful when making an argument to managers or funders about investing in trustworthy channels and verification.

How I'll use it: In the guide's "Trust & Institutional Response" section to motivate why verification is also an economic opportunity for credible media.

What Are AI Hallucinations? — IBM

IBM: Explainers on model hallucination

Summary: A clear technical explainer describing why large language models sometimes "make things up," including causes and mitigation approaches.

Why it matters: Understanding hallucinations helps teams set realistic expectations for AI outputs and design verification layers into workflows.

How I'll use it: As the foundational technical primer in the "How it works" subsection of the guide.

Milton Wolf Seminar — Media & Diplomacy (UPenn)

Explore the seminar

Summary: Academic seminars connecting media manipulation, diplomacy, and international communications; useful for policy and global context.

Why it matters: Places the technical issue within geopolitics and public diplomacy—helpful for policy-focused career paths.

How I'll use it: In the "Policy & Global Impacts" section and for recommending academic events to follow.

AI Model Hallucinations: Risks — SeniorExecutive

Read the article

Summary: Business-oriented discussion of hallucinations' operational, reputational, and legal risks, plus governance suggestions for executives.

Why it matters: Bridges the gap between technical failure modes and board-level concerns—useful when advocating for investment in verification and vendor controls.

How I'll use it: For the "Business & Governance" checklist and vendor due-diligence recommendations.

I Tested 12 AI Humanizers—Bypassing Detection (Skywork)

Read the report

Summary: Hands-on evaluations of “humanizer” tools that rework AI-generated text to evade detection, documenting methods and ethical concerns.

Why it matters: Demonstrates the detection-evasion arms race and shows why multilayered defenses (education + technical + policy) are needed.

How I'll use it: In the "Tools & Detection" section to motivate a combined approach beyond labeling alone.

Mini Quiz — Can you spot the red flags?

A quick interactive exercise to practice spotting risky signals associated with AI-generated content. This is intentionally lightweight: designed for engagement and reflection.

Q1. You receive a short video of a public figure making a controversial statement. The clip has slightly odd lip movements and very smooth background audio. What should you do first?
Q2. An email asks you to approve a wire transfer with an audio clip of your CFO saying to do it. The voice matches closely. What is the safest action?

After trying the quiz: consider training your team on a verification protocol (a one-minute callback confirmation for high-value requests).
